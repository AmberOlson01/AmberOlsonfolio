Psychology Honors Thesis

My thesis examins using games as an alternative to self-report personality assessments to predict job performance. My methods includes a self-report survey items on the Big Five Personality traits and data from my participant's Grand Theft Auto player profile. As of now, I predict that game assessments will be more accurate in predicting job performance. as compared to self-report survey items.

A.D.V.A.N.C.E. CSUSB

Amber Olson spent her summer in an intensive10-week research program funded by the CSUSB Office of Student Engagement. This team investigated Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) disparities in recruitment, retention, promotion, and tenure for female and minority faculty members on the CSUSB campus. This research, led by Dr. Donna Garcia, will be used to apply for a series of grants, including a one million dollar ADVANCE grant from the National Science Foundation (NSF). The team completed literature searches, coded large data files, developed stimuli for an implicit bias test, conducted experimental research with human participants, developed a surveys via Qualtrics, and gained experience with research software such as SPSS and Zotero. Through these activities, the team identified some gender and ethnic disparities on the CSUSB campus in the recruitment, hiring, retention, promotion and tenure processes. These disparities are particularly pronounced in the STEM disciplines at CSUSB. The focus of our future research will be to determine the sources of inequities at CSUSB and develop an intervention for them.
